# NEXUS Calibration — Start Here

Answer these 8 questions honestly. There are no right or wrong answers — the goal is to map how you actually process information, not how you think you should.

---

**Q1. When you receive new information, you prefer:**
- [A] A step-by-step explanation with full context
- [B] The conclusion first, details only if needed
- [C] A visual structure or diagram

**Q2. When a problem has multiple valid solutions, you:**
- [A] Evaluate all options before deciding
- [B] Apply the first that seems to work, adjust later
- [C] Look for the solution that solves the class of problem, not just this case

**Q3. How do you prefer to receive negative feedback?**
- [A] With context and emotional validation first
- [B] Direct: what failed and why
- [C] Just the data and the correction, no narrative

**Q4. When working on something complex, you tend to:**
- [A] Work in order, step by step
- [B] See the full system first, then execute
- [C] Start where curiosity pulls you, connect later

**Q5. How much social context do you need in a productive interaction?**
- [A] Quite a bit — I want to know who I'm talking to and feel rapport
- [B] Some — a bit of context but not excess
- [C] None — just the information, the rest is noise

**Q6. When an analogy explains something technical, which is most useful?**
- [A] A everyday story ("it's like when you cook...")
- [B] A structural metaphor ("it works like a distribution network")
- [C] A one-to-one domain map ("X in physics = Y in data science")

**Q7. Your natural reaction to ambiguity:**
- [A] I adapt well — ambiguity gives me flexibility
- [B] I tolerate it but prefer clarity when possible
- [C] It blocks me — I need specificity to operate well

**Q8. When you finish a project, what feels like success?**
- [A] The result met the objective
- [B] The process was efficient AND the result was good
- [C] The solution is elegant, scalable, and I understand why it works

---

## Scoring

Count your answers:

| Mostly A | Mostly B | Mostly C |
|----------|----------|----------|
| → NEXUS-L or NEXUS-C | → NEXUS-A or NEXUS-E | → NEXUS-D |

Go to `profiles/` and open the closest match. Paste the system prompt into your LLM and run a 10-minute calibration session with it. Ask it to explain something you know well — then adjust density and format until the output matches how you think.

Your profile is not fixed. It evolves with use.
